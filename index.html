About SITHELO NPO

For over 16 years, we have walked alongside women, youth and children in Durban — igniting hope, building resilience and transforming lives through purpose-driven community action.

Who We Are

SITHELO NPO is a community-driven non-profit organisation dedicated to creating lasting change by empowering women, youth, and vulnerable children. We believe that every individual deserves the opportunity to live with dignity, realise their potential, and contribute meaningfully to their community.

Our work focuses on addressing the challenges of poverty, inequality, unemployment, and social exclusion through practical, sustainable programmes. From skills development and educational support to mentorship, community outreach, and access to health and social services, we equip individuals with the knowledge, confidence, and opportunities they need to build brighter futures.

At the heart of everything we do is a simple belief: empowered people create empowered communities. By investing in education, leadership, and economic empowerment, we are helping to build resilient families, stronger communities, and a future where everyone has the opportunity to thrive.

Together with our partners, volunteers, and supporters, we are committed to creating hope, inspiring change, and making a meaningful impact—one life, one family, and one community at a time.

What Drives Us

  • Women deserve economic independence and social equality
  • Youth need direction, mentorship and real opportunity
  • Children require safety, nutrition and access to education
  • Communities grow stronger when they support one another
  • Skills are the most durable form of empowerment
  • Every voice in the community matters and deserves to be heard

Our Vision

A society in which every woman stands in her power, every young person has a clear and hopeful path forward, and every child grows up in safety, dignity and love.

Our Mission

To ignite and sustain hope in communities through women empowerment, youth development, children's welfare and skills training — creating a ripple of positive change that extends across generations.

"Sivusa Ithemba Lembokodo — reviving the hope of a strong woman. This is not just our name. It is our promise."

Our Programmes

Discover the specific initiatives we run to empower our community. From skills training to youth mentorship, find out how you can get involved.

Women Empowerment

Leadership training, entrepreneurship support and advocacy that helps women claim their rightful place in society. This programme focuses on building both economic independence and personal confidence.

Eligibility: Women aged 18+ from Durban South
Schedule: Bi-weekly workshops (Saturdays)
Apply or Volunteer

Youth Development

Mentorship, life skills and opportunity pathways that equip young people to become confident leaders of tomorrow. We partner with local schools and businesses to provide career guidance.

Eligibility: Youth aged 14-25
Schedule: After-school sessions (Wed & Fri)
Apply or Volunteer

Children's Welfare

Safe spaces, nutritional support and holistic care that protect children's dignity, safety and potential. We provide daily meals and a secure environment for vulnerable children.

Eligibility: Children aged 4-13
Schedule: Mon-Fri, 14:00 - 17:00
Apply or Volunteer

Community Engagement

Neighbourhood outreach, awareness campaigns and local partnership building that creates lasting social bonds. We host monthly community dialogues and clean-up drives.

Eligibility: Open to all community members
Schedule: Last Saturday of every month
Apply or Volunteer

School Drives

Stationery collections, uniform donations and reading programmes ensuring no child goes without the tools to learn. Distributed twice a year ahead of school terms.

Eligibility: Learners from partnered schools
Schedule: January & July distributions
Support this Drive

Skills & Trade

Vocational training in crafts, trades and digital skills — giving community members tools for sustainable livelihoods. Includes our popular 6-week sewing and digital literacy bootcamps.

Eligibility: Unemployed individuals aged 18+
Schedule: 6-week modules (Mon-Wed mornings)
Apply or Volunteer
